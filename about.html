About

I’m Josh Martino, an Information Technology major with a Robotics minor at James Madison University. I’m most interested in the space where software and systems meet real-world constraints: building reliable environments, automating repetitive work, and making technical setups easy for other people to reproduce.

I work primarily in Linux and Python, with experience in networking fundamentals, SQL, and containerized workflows using Docker. In team projects, I usually take ownership of clarity and delivery—tightening documentation, standardizing setup steps, and turning “it works on my machine” into something that works for everyone.

What I’m focused on

  • Reliable setups: Linux-first workflows, clean installs, and repeatable environments.
  • Automation: Python scripts and tooling that reduce manual effort and prevent mistakes.
  • Documentation: onboarding guides, troubleshooting steps, and clear handoffs for teams.
  • Systems + robotics support: practical work that helps hardware/software teams move faster.
